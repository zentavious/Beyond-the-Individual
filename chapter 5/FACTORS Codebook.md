# FACTORS Codebook

**Goal:** Leverage FACTORS (Functions for Action with Communities: Teaching, Overseeing, Reciprocating, Sustaining) theoretical framework for a directed content analysis of online community guidelines for research.

**Sub goal(s):** 1) Identify exemplars/use cases for FACTORS, 2) identify exemplars for guidelines template.

**Limitations:** Online community guidelines for research engagement are a passive strategy for overseeing bright-lines. Therefore, we expect much of this analysis to bias Overseeing. Sampling methods for identifying online communities also introduce bias.

## How to apply codes
Community guidelines are defined as rules or resources that relate to research or research methods explicitly. This includes mentions of research methods like surveys or scraping. For each subcode, identify all evidence justifying its label. To do so, add an open code with the format `[[abbreviation]]:[[description]]`. Apply open codes to individual units of meaning to describe emergent concepts, practices, or expectations related to ethical online community research that are not represented by the codebook.

## Function Sub-Codes

### Teaching
Guidelines with content focused on sensitizing, educating, or socializing researchers to the community.

#### Sub Codes:
* **Socializing Research:** Specifying values, norms, or goals of the community to researchers, including expectations for members that also apply to researchers. This includes explaining why the community has their own guidelines/rules for research, or justifying a rule or expectation using participation norms or community values.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"The community expects everyone to participate in good faith"* (**Code(s):** Communicating Shared Understanding of Values)
        * *"Attribution is an important component of how members share content in our community and the same goes for research using members data"* (**Code(s):** Communicating Shared Understanding of Values, Attribution & Acknowledgement, Setting Boundaries)
        * *"Please read our general community rules before starting research"* (**Code(s):** Communicating Shared Understanding of Values, Setting Boundaries)

* **Active Self Learning:** Specific pathways for researchers to learn about the community by doing. This includes any mention of researchers engaging or contributing to the community as members, or in the same way members do.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Research posts must come from an account with 6 months of post history in the community."* (**Code(s):** Active Self Learning, Setting Boundaries)
        * *"The best way to learn is by participating in our community"* (**Code(s):** Active Self Learning)

* **Active Learning from Members:** Recommendations or processes to support researchers engaging directly with members or organizers of the community to learn or get feedback. This includes general invitations to reach out to specific people or ways to post questions.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Before submitting a public post, have someone familiar with our community provide first-pass feedback first"* (**Code(s):** Active Self Learning, Setting Boundaries)
        * *"Research in our community should have at least one member included as a co-author"* (**Code(s):** Active Self Learning, Setting Boundaries)
        * *"If you have questions about this document or any rules, please reach out to X"* (**Code(s):** Active Self Learning)

### Overseeing
Focuses on guardrails, gatekeeping, and enforcement of expectations with specifics about rules or guidelines.

#### Sub Codes:
* **Setting Boundaries:** Specific expectations about what researchers can and cannot do within the community. This includes recommendations that explicitly mention things researchers are encouraged to do or not do, even if it isn’t an enforceable rule.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Please do not scrape posts"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries)
        * *"You may observe and post publicly, but do not private messages members directly"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries)
        * *"Researcher profiles must disclose their affiliation"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries)
        * *"Research should provide some insights back to the community"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Sharing Research Outcomes)
        * *"Our community appreciates diverse perspectives, therefore researchers should avoid discrediting specific members' perspectives."* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Socializing Research)

* **Community Approval:** What community members want to review, approve, or control before researchers can access specific community spaces or activities. This doesn’t need to be enforceable, just a stated expectation for active review before a researcher action.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Moderator approval is required before posting recruitment messages"* (**Code(s):** Community Approval, Setting Boundaries)
        * *"If you want to include quotes from our members you should obtain explicit permission"* (**Code(s):** Community Approval, Setting Boundaries)
        * *"Survey should include a ‘reviewed by’ clause from a member familiar with our community"* (**Code(s):** Community Approval, Setting Boundaries)

* **Encouraging Compliance:** How the community monitors compliance, responds to violations, implements consequences/repercussions, or incentives for compliance.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Members should report all violations to moderators"* (**Code(s):** Encouraging Compliance)
        * *"Auto-moderator will flag and remove survey posts"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Encouraging Compliance)
        * *"Breaking these rules results in permanent ban and notification to your institution"* (**Code(s):** Encouraging Compliance)
        * *"Before publishing, we’d love a summary of your results. We may be able to provide advice or support to help improve your findings"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Encouraging Compliance, Community Approval)

### Reciprocating
Focuses on the community as a beneficiary of research with specifics about what or how to benefit. These guidelines contain content related to positive outcomes.

#### Sub Codes:
* **Attribution & Acknowledgement:** Details how or what to give credit to the community or its members for their research contributions. This includes notes about compensation and citations.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Consider acknowledging individual contributors by username (with permission)"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Attribution & Acknowledgement, Community Approval)
        * *"Include a mention of our community in your acknowledgements section"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Attribution & Acknowledgement)
        * *"Monetary compensation is not appropriate in our community"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Attribution & Acknowledgement)

* **Sharing Research Outcomes:** Details processes or expectations for sharing research findings back to the community in accessible formats.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"You are required to share a summary of your findings with moderators"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Sharing Research Outcomes)
        * *"After publishing, we’d love to know about your findings. We may be interested in promoting your work via a post or AMA"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Approval, Sharing Research Outcomes, Encouraging Compliance)
        * *"Please publish in open access journals so our members have access when you share links"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Sharing Research Outcomes)

* **Community Benefits:** Justifying a rule or stated expectations or a set of guidelines for research by mention of its benefits or use to the community. This includes recommendations for researchers for outcomes or topics to pursue or avoid.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Research should be a relevant topic to our community, but not under the premise of curing members. Instead focus on improving the lives of members under this condition."* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Benefits)
        * *"Survey results are generally not valuable to our community and should be avoided"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Benefits)

### Sustaining
Focuses on managing the collective load or minimizing the harm of research on the community. These guidelines are generally related to negative impacts.

#### Sub Codes:
* **Community Impacts:** Justifying a rule or stated expectations for research by mention of the cost, burden, or harm on the community. This includes mentioning things to preserve, minimize, or be cautious about impacting.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Avoid methods that take away members’ time from participating in our community"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Impacts)
        * *"To protect the privacy of our members, surveys shouldn’t ask for unnecessary demographic information from our members"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Impacts)
        * *"Organizer approval helps ensure that determining the legitimacy of researchers doesn’t fall only on members"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Impacts, Community Approval)
        * *"Organizer review helps ensure your results fairly represent our community"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Impacts)

* **Regulating Concurrent Research:** Managing how much research can be done at one time. This includes mention of volume, size, time, or frequency of an active research project. This also includes mentions of boundaries in relation to past or concurrent projects.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Recruitment posts are only allowed on Fridays"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Regulating Concurrent Research)
        * *"One survey post per approval. Duplicates will be removed."* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Regulating Concurrent Research, Encouraging Compliance)
        * *"Avoid repeating topics or questions from prior research in our community"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Regulating Concurrent Research)

* **Research Archiving:** Recording, documenting, or maintaining records of previous, ongoing, or planned research conducted or to be conducted with the community. This doesn’t include one-off examples of past research.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"Here is a list of past research in our community:..."* (**Code(s):** Research Archiving)
        * *"Researchers should register the title, purpose and time in our research thread"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Research Archiving, Standardizing Research Engagement)
        * *"After completion, notify organizers to add your research to our Past Studies wiki page"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Research Archiving)
        * *"Include a tag “Research” in your community notification post for future reference"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Research Archiving, Standardizing Research Engagement)

* **Standardizing Research Engagement:** Rules or expectations about information to include in community research proposals or member engagement. These ask researchers to follow a standardized or similar pattern. This must be a method to follow, template to follow, or set of explicit information to include in a proposal or post.
    * *Examples:*
        * *"When submitting a research request, include academic email, IRB number, and study purpose."* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Approval, Standardizing Research Engagement)
        * *"Recruitment posts should indicate moderator approval and how study data will be used."* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Standardizing Research Engagement)
        * *"Researchers are required to go through an interview process before engaging with members"* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Approval, Standardizing Research Engagement)
    * *Non-Example:*
        * *"All community research requires written consent."* (**Code(s):** Setting Boundaries, Community Approval)
